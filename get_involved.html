Get involved

People are able to get involved in many ways. It just takes an act of will and kindness to help protect our marine life.

At Shark Attack, we believe that everyone can play a role in protecting marine life and preserving our oceans. Whether you're passionate about hands-on work, advocacy, or education, we offer a variety of ways for you to make an impact. Our volunteer community is at the core of everything we do, and no effort is too small when it comes to creating a healthier planet.

Ways to Get Involved

1. Join a Beach Clean-Up Help remove harmful plastics and debris from coastlines to prevent pollution from threatening marine wildlife. Our clean-ups take place regularly in coastal areas—grab your gloves and join us!
2. Assist in Coral Reef Restoration Support our efforts to restore damaged reefs by planting coral fragments and monitoring their growth. This hands-on experience is perfect for those who want to learn more about marine ecosystems.

3. Spread Awareness Become an advocate for marine conservation! Host an awareness event, share our message on social media, or organize a local fundraiser to support Shark Attack’s initiatives.

4. Educate Your Community Lead workshops or presentations in schools, libraries, or community centers to teach others about ocean conservation and how they can help.

5. Intern or Volunteer with Us Long-Term Looking for deeper involvement? Join our volunteer leadership team or apply for an internship. These roles offer a chance to gain hands-on experience in environmental work and help shape the future of Shark Attack.

Why Volunteer?

  • Direct Impact on marine ecosystems.
  • Meet Like-Minded People who share your passion for the environment.
  • Gain Experience in environmental conservation, education, and advocacy.
  • Create Change in your community and beyond.

Ready To Make A Difference?

No experience is needed—just your time and passion! Check out our Upcoming Events page for ways to get started, or sign up to be notified about new volunteer opportunities. Together, we can protect marine life and ensure our oceans thrive for generations to come. Dive in with us and become part of the movement today!
